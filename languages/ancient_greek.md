# Illiad
μῆνιν ἄειδε, θεά, Πηληιάδεω Ἀχιλῆος : goddess, sing the anger of Achilles, Peleus' son
  Ἀχιλλεύς : Achilles
  Πηλεύς : Peleus <father of Achilles>
  -ῐ́δης : son of
  μῆνῐς {f3} : rage / wrath <mostly of the gods>
  ἀείδω : to sing
  θεός θεᾱ́  : god / godess
οὐλομένην, ἣ μυρί’ Ἀχαιοῖς ἄλγε’ ἔθηκε : the accursed, who put countless sorrows upon the Achaeans
  Ἀχαιός : Achaean <from the region of Achaea. In Homeric Greek, this refers to Greek in general>
  οὐλόμενος : accursed
  ὅς ἥ ὅ : who ; which / that
  μῡρῐ́ος : countless
  ἄλγος : pain / sorrow
  τῐ́θημῐ : to put / to place
πολλὰς δ’ ἰφθίμους ψυχὰς Ἄϊδι προΐαψεν : and sent many strong souls to Hades
  ᾍδης : Hades
  δέ : but ; and
  πολῠ́ς πολλή πολῠ́  : many <in plural forms>
  ἴφθῑμος #Epic : good <often translated as 'strong', it was used as epiphet for humans (incl. women which is why strong may not be the best translation always), gods, animals and implied a positive moral quality>
  ψῡχή : soul
  προϊᾰ́πτω #Epic : to send forth
ἡρώων, αὐτοὺς δὲ ἑλώρια τεῦχε κύνεσσιν : of heroes, and made them preys for dogs
  ἥρως : hero <in Epic, this refers to any combatant of the Trojan war, later to noteworthy human or demigods who had shrines>
  αὐτός : he / she / it / they <without article>
  ἑλώριον : booty
  τεύχω : to make ; to cause
  κῠ́ων {mf3; κῠνός} : dog
οἰωνοῖσί τε πᾶσι· Διὸς δ’ ἐτελείετο βουλή : and to all the birds, the will of Zeus was being accomplished
  Ζεύς {m3; Διός} : Zeus
  οἰωνός {m2} : large bird / bird of prey
  τε : and <weaker than και>
  τελέω : to bring about
  βουλή {f1} : will
ἐξ οὗ δὴ τὰ πρῶτα διαστήτην ἐρίσαντε : after which they first separated, having quarelled
  ἐκ / ἐξ[V] [GEN] : out of ; after
  οὗ : of him / of her
  δή :  but ; and ; =particle <various meanings: temporal (now, already), emphasis (truly), specificity (exactly), irony, of all people>
  ὁ {ἡ τό τώ_ οἱ αἱ τᾰ́} : the <was rare in Epic but became common in Attic> ; that #Epic ; he / she / it #Epic ; who / which / that
  πρῶτᾰ : in the first place 
  πρῶτος : first
  δῐίστημῐ : to set apart
  ἐρίζω : to struggle
Ἀτρεΐδης τε ἄναξ ἀνδρῶν καὶ δῖος Ἀχιλλεύς : son of Atreus, lord of men and divine Achilles
  Ἀτρεύς : Atreus
  ἄνᾰξ : lord / king <often referring to Apollo> ; master / owner
  ᾰ̓νήρ {m3; ᾰ̓νδρός} : man
  δῖος #Epic : divine
Τίς τάρ σφωε θεῶν ἔριδι ξυνέηκε μάχεσθαι; : which one of the gods made these two eager to fight?
  τῐ́ς {τί} : who <in masculine and feminine> ; what <with neuter>
  τάρ : and so
  ἔρῐς {f3; ἔρῐδος} : strife / quarrel
  σῠνῑ́ημῐ !ξῠνῑ́ημῐ : to send together
  μάχομαι : to make war
  σφωέ #Epic : they two
